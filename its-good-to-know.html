It's good to know

It's good to know where you're going.

Jan 2018, I was as clueless as a fresh graduate out of college about what I wanted to do and where I wanted to go next.

My personal life had fallen apart. I'd quit my job. I'd saved some money and I'd taken some time off. 3 years to be precise. This was the longest I'd gone since my 11 year career stint at various companies where I held positions ranging from a Design Engineer to a Global IP head.

I consulted for a while and tried staying active in my line of work but I couldn't find the motivation I once had to keep going. So what next?

I checked off a few things in my bucket-list. Wrote a book. Tried to find purpose. Tried to imagine a life I wanted. Found love. Found peace. But no luck with work.

I had a faint idea of what I wanted and I wrote it down.
1. Build tech that helps people.
2. Travel
3. Set-up a school in the North-Eastern mountains.
4. Live there forever.

I knew the first task in this list, if done right, could potentially set the course for the rest. So I got busy. What could I build? How could I help? What did I need for myself that others could use too?

So I made another list.
1. I wanted clarity about where I was going.
2. I wanted to live with purpose everyday.
3. I wanted to take accountability for acheiving my goals.
4. I wanted to work for it day in and day out.

Just writing it all down, helped me feel better and see better. It was evident, I wanted to build Clymb, an app to help people write down their goals, break it down, and go after it every single day. Great, sounded easy, except, it wasn't.

I did not know how to code and I could not afford to have it outsourced. I had to build it by myself.

So I set myself new goals spanning the next one year.
1. Learn to code.
2. Build three fun apps and publish on the App Store.
3. Build Clymb.

Amazing things happen when you write down your goals and follow through.

I enrolled myself to a course in Udemy. Stuck through the most frusrating days and times with code. Found great appreciation for everything open source. And I don't know what I'd have done without Stack Overflow.

Over the course of the next one year, I built and published 3 free apps - AdCopy, Memaw, and BeetrootInn to the App Store.

And I finally built and published Clymb as a freemium app.

July 19th, 2019. I managed to clear App Review and Clymb app was finally on the App Store. Amazing things can happen when you write down your goals and follow through.

My next goal? To spread the word and get it into the hands of as many people as possible. Will I succeed? I'll write about it in my next post.

Best,
Chethan Kamath
Founder, Clymb
